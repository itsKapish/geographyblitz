References

These additonal resources are beneficial for MYP and DP Students learning about Geography, Geopolitics, History, Economics and Humanities which are part of Individuals and Societies. I have used these resources not only for research to create Geography Blitz but also in my MYP Studies and these are authentic, accurate and provide useeful information to a high degree.


Hover and Click over the Title headings of the resources to get redirected to the resource web-links.


  • Omniatlas

    Omniatlas is an interactive atlas to view major events in World History, showcasing maps of specific regions with Next and Previous buttons, which you can toggle and click to move forward or backward in time, seeing how borders of nations changed and revolutions and wars progressed. For MYP Individuals and Societies, This resource is especially crucial for analysing the territorial changes and major events of World War I (On All Fronts), World War 2 (On All Fronts), Napoleonic Wars (and other Wars), Revolutions and the Cold War.

  • IB Economics with Brad Cartwright

    Brad Cartwright is facilitator teaching IB Economics in Santiago, Chile. He has graduated from Duke University and is a former Peace Corps volunteer. As he has travelled over 45 countries, his insights in his videos related to DP Economics (extremely benefical for MYP also) help break down complex Economics topics into simple and digestable concepts. Through the use of Case Studies, structured Content and Examples, he helps IB Students hone their understanding in Economics and develop in Economics. For MYP Individuals and Societies, this resource is crucial for Growth & Development and Market Systems & Economies. The link directs you to his YouTube channel.

  • Armchair Historian

    Griffin Johnsen, the founder of The Armchair Historian LLC, a private media production company, creates videos about major Historical events in Human History, intricately explaining the events through the usage of animations. His insights provide multiple perspectives on a single event, such as the perspective of multiple nations during World War 2. For MYP Individuals and Societies, this resource is crucial for World War I, World War 2 and Superpowers and Conflicts. The link directs you to his YouTube channel.

  • Caspian Report

    Caspian Report is a YouTube Channel with hosts explaining recent geopolitical trends by cutting through all the noise, providing information from all perspectives and evaluating real-life crises and policies. This unbiased source is optimal for gaining examples while writing Criteria C and D content during assessments. For MYP Individuals and Societies, this resource is crucial for World War I, World War 2 and Superpowers and Conflicts. The link directs you to his YouTube channel.

  • Think School

    Think School is an education start-up to teach business, geopolitics and economics using modern examples and real-life scenarios, especially centralized around India, in order to gain an in-depth understanding of content and concept. For MYP Individuals and Societies, this resource is crucial for Market Systems & Economies, Growth and Development, Impacts of Tourism, Rights and Protests and Superpowers and Conflicts. The link directs you to his YouTube channel.

  • History.com

    History.com is an organization which creates documentaries and website articles. Due to the fact that this website is directed by television producers, I do not recommend using the Documentaries as a reliable source. However, the Website Articles, especially pertaining to the World Wars and Immigration, provide a key insight into important historical events and give descriptive overviews regarding events. I do not recommend studying from this source if you are already well-versed with concepts that you want to research more on. For MYP Individuals and Societies, this resource is crucial for World War 1, World War 2 and Rights and Protests.

  • Our World in Data

    Our World in Data is a reliable and accurate data source which provides clear and interactive statistics across topics such as Climate Change, Population and Demographics, Wars and Tensions and Development Indices. These valid statistics are backed by explanations through articles and provide in-depth statistical analysis by comparing indvidual countries. For MYP Individuals and Societies, these resource is crucial for all units and topics.

  • Epic History

    Epic History is a YouTube channel which depicts events from rise and fall of empires to global conflicts and revolutions. Using intricate animations and detail in his videos, the narrator explains historical events chronologically, creating a narrative experience. For MYP Individuals and Societies, this source is crucial for World War I and World War II.

  • United Nations Organization

    Needing no introduction, the UNO is the perfect resource for reports, case studies and precise and reliable statistics with accurate data representations. Although this source might be obvious to use, I feel that it's undervalued as their financial reports and reports on tourism and human development indices provide invaluable information for providing effective current (real-life) examples in assessments. This resource can also be used for learning about ongoing conflicts, wars and studying rights and protests whilst learning how international organizations play a major role in resolving these conflicts and promoting Human Rights.
